Commission Holds Environmental Summit in Chisinau

26 November 2025 • News • Chisinau • 6 min read

Experts and representatives of the Parties met in Chisinau to review progress on water management, discuss new monitoring approaches, and coordinate upcoming joint activities and projects.

The meeting focused on the Commission’s current priorities and the upcoming work programme. Participants exchanged updates on monitoring results, data interoperability, and reporting formats, with particular attention to improving the accessibility of public information.

Key outcomes

  • Agreement on the next steps for harmonising monitoring indicators and reporting cycles.
  • Preliminary alignment on data exchange requirements for GIS/HIS modules.
  • Planning of joint field visits and capacity-building activities for 2026.

Background

The Dniester River Basin requires coordinated action among riparian stakeholders. The Commission supports cooperation through shared monitoring, joint initiatives, and dissemination of official information, publications, and public awareness materials.

“The Commission’s work depends on consistent data and transparent communication. This meeting helps us advance both.”
